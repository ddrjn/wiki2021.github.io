Introduction

By introducing more people to the scientific world, we create a diverse, welcoming community interactingwithin itself. We recognize the importance of includingmore people in science, following the overall goal of IGEM â€” creating an open, collaborative community

Estonia is not a uniform country in terms of languages the general population speaks. Except for Estonian, some people only speak Russian or English. To reach more people, we were able to do our workshops in all three languages

According to the data of the 2011 census, the primary minority language is Russian, with 372,480 speakers, i.e., 29.6% of the population. The total number of citizens whose mother tongue is not Estonian was 22,103 people or 1.3% of the population in 2011. Additionally, we observed that sometimes people who speak both languages could be more eager to participate in our workshops if we communicate with them in Russian

There were 5236 international degree students in Estonia studying in the previous 2020/2021 academic year. International degree students make up around 12% of the students in Estonia. Additionally to that number, some people stayed after their studies or immigrated to Estonia without Estonian or Russian language knowledge

To engage all possible groups, we conducted the workshops in 3 languages, so participants could feel comfortable learning new things without the language barrier

Moreover, we are looking forward to further collaborations with other teams by providing them with our workshop protocols. By sharing our experience we expand the outreach.

Introductory laboratory course

By eliminating barriers to participation, every willing student will be able to get involved in the iGEM lab later, regardless of their previous experience. Our students come from all over the world with different school backgrounds, speaking different languages. No matter what they did and who they are, we are making it possible for them to be involved in this amazing scientific journey

Our team conducted an introductory lab course for first-year students in Science and Technology, introducing some techniques used in biology. Students have learned basic practical skills, and we are waiting for them as next year iGEM participants
